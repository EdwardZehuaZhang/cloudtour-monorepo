If you've seen a CloudTour experience and wondered how it looks so lifelike, the answer is Gaussian splatting. Let's break down what it is and why it matters.

## What Is Gaussian Splatting?

At its core, Gaussian splatting (formally "3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Radiance Field Rendering") is a technique for reconstructing 3D scenes from photographs. It was introduced in 2023 and quickly became one of the most exciting developments in computer graphics.

Instead of traditional polygon meshes, the scene is represented as millions of tiny 3D Gaussians — think of them as soft, colored blobs in space. Each Gaussian has:

- A **position** in 3D space
- A **color** (with view-dependent effects)
- An **opacity** value
- A **covariance matrix** defining its shape and orientation

## Why Not NeRF?

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) pioneered photorealistic novel view synthesis, but they come with drawbacks:

- **Slow rendering** — NeRFs require expensive ray marching
- **Long training times** — hours to days per scene
- **Hard to edit** — the scene is locked inside a neural network

Gaussian splatting solves all three:

- **Real-time rendering** at 30+ FPS using rasterization
- **Training in minutes** on a consumer GPU
- **Explicit representation** — you can edit, merge, and manipulate individual Gaussians

## File Formats We Support

CloudTour supports the three most common Gaussian splat formats:

- **PLY** — the original format from the research paper
- **SPLAT** — a compact binary format optimized for web delivery
- **SPZ** — a compressed format for even smaller file sizes

## The Future

Gaussian splatting is evolving rapidly. Dynamic scenes, relightable splatting, and higher compression ratios are all active areas of research. CloudTour is built to support these advances as they mature.
