The real estate industry was an early adopter of virtual tours, but most agents are still using technology from a decade ago. Flat panoramas and auto-rotating slideshows don't sell homes — they check a box.

## The Limitations of Panorama Tours

Traditional 360-degree tours suffer from several problems:

- **No depth perception** — rooms look distorted and unnatural
- **Fixed viewpoints** — you can only look from where the camera was placed
- **Stitching artifacts** — visible seams between images break immersion
- **No spatial understanding** — it's hard to gauge room sizes and layouts

## Why Gaussian Splatting Changes Everything

Gaussian splatting captures the actual 3D geometry of a space. Buyers can:

- Orbit freely around rooms, not just look from fixed points
- Judge room proportions accurately thanks to true 3D depth
- Explore architectural details from any angle
- Experience the flow between rooms naturally

## Getting Started with CloudTour for Real Estate

Setting up a property tour on CloudTour is straightforward:

1. **Capture** your property using a smartphone or DSLR (we recommend 50-200 photos per room)
2. **Process** the photos through a Gaussian splatting tool to generate .ply or .splat files
3. **Upload** scenes to CloudTour and connect them with waypoints
4. **Add hotspots** highlighting features — kitchen appliances, views, storage
5. **Publish** and share the tour link with prospective buyers

## Results That Speak

Early adopters report that listings with Gaussian splat tours receive significantly more engagement than those with traditional photography alone. The immersive quality keeps viewers exploring longer, building a genuine connection with the space before they ever visit in person.
